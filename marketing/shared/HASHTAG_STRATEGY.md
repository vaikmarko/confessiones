## üì± Platform-Specific Strategy

### Instagram:
- **Number:** 10-15 hashtags per post
- **Placement:** First comment (keeps caption clean) OR end of caption
- **Best practice:** Mix all 4 volume types
- **Refresh:** Change hashtags every post (rotate sets)
- **Stories:** 1-3 hashtags only (less spammy)
- **Reels:** Use trending + niche combo

### Facebook:
- **Number:** 1-3 hashtags max (FB users don't like many hashtags)
- **Placement:** End of caption
- **Best practice:** Only use 1-2 most relevant
- **Examples:** #ChristianLife #Faith

### Twitter/X:
- **Number:** 2-3 hashtags
- **Placement:** Integrated in tweet text naturally
- **Best practice:** Use trending Christian hashtags
- **Examples:** #ChristianTwitter #FaithTwitter

## üîç Hashtag Research Process

### Finding New Hashtags:

1. **Instagram Search:**
   - Search a hashtag (e.g., #ChristianLife)
   - Look at "Related" hashtags at top
   - Note volume (number of posts)
   - Check recency (are posts recent? If not, dead hashtag)

2. **Competitor Analysis:**
   - Find 5-10 Christian accounts similar to yours
   - Look at their top posts
   - See which hashtags they use
   - Test their hashtags

3. **Hashtag Tools:**
   - All Hashtag (allhashta.com)
   - Display Purposes (displaypurposes.com)
   - Seekmetrics

4. **Track Performance:**
   - Use Instagram Insights
   - See which hashtags drove reach
   - Double down on winners

## üìä Hashtag Performance Tracking

### Track These Metrics:

**Reach per Hashtag:**
- Instagram Insights ‚Üí Post ‚Üí "From Hashtags"
- Shows how many people discovered post via hashtags
- Goal: >10% of total reach from hashtags

**Engagement Rate:**
- Likes + Comments + Saves / Reach from Hashtags
- Winning hashtag = high engagement from that hashtag

**Top Performers:**
- After 2 weeks, analyze which hashtags drove most traffic
- Create "Winners" set - use more often
- Drop "Losers" - low/no reach
